Tourists in Kazakhstan prefer hotels without stars - study

16th May, 2022

Tourists in Kazakhstan prefer staying in hotels without any category. Three million people chose this type of accommodation throughout 2021, Tengritravel.kz reports with reference to Finprom .

In total, about five and a half million people stayed in places of residence for the entire past year, including foreign tourists and residents of the country. Of these, more than 560 thousand people stay in five-star hotels, about 554 thousand people in four-star hotels, almost 245 thousand guests in three-star hotels, 17.8 thousand people in two-star hotels, and three and a half thousand people in one-star hotels.

Almaty became the leader in terms of the number of guests served: 1.2 million people, or 22.3 percent of the total. At the same time, the number of customers grew by 65.5 percent over the year. The leading regions also included Nur-Sultan (751,000 people, with an increase of 78.4 percent) and Almaty region (609,000 people, that is, an increase of 26.1 percent compared to 2020).

Least of all visitors were served in the Atyrau region. Here the number of visitors decreased immediately by 45.2 percent - up to 46.3 thousand people. The decrease in the number of visitors served was observed only in this region.

The volume of services provided by accommodation places in 2021 reached 109 billion tenge in monetary terms, that is, 63.1 percent more than a year earlier. Most services on this indicator were rendered in Almaty: 27.9 billion tenge, annual growth - 66.1 percent. The top three regions also included the capital (20.3 billion tenge, plus 75.4 percent) and Akmola region (10.5 billion tenge, plus 63.9 percent). Least of all services were rendered in the Kyzylorda region - only by 1.1 billion tenge.

Most often, guests preferred hotels with a restaurant. 2.7 million people stayed in such establishments last year, which is 62.8 percent more than a year earlier. The choice of tourists also fell on places of accommodation without restaurants (1.7 million people), one-story bungalows, rural houses, cottages, small houses and apartments (673 thousand people). The top 5 popular places also included holiday homes and tourist camps.
